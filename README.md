# E-commerce-Customer-Intelligence-Platform
E-commerce Customer Intelligence Platform analyzing 100K+ transactions with Python, SQL, Pandas, and Power BI. Features RFM customer segmentation, cohort analysis, CLV tracking, product affinity analysis, and interactive dashboards for data-driven business insights.

## Project Overview

This notebook walks through an end-to-end customer analytics process, including:

* Customer exploration and geographic analysis
* Transaction data engineering
* Revenue and sales trend analysis
* RFM-based customer segmentation
* Machine learning customer clustering using K-Means
* Cohort retention analysis
* Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) estimation
* Product affinity and cross-sell analysis
* Executive KPI reporting and business recommendations

## Dataset

**Source:** Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset

**Time Period:** September 2016 – August 2018

The dataset contains customer, order, payment, review, and product information from a large Brazilian e-commerce marketplace.

## Key Analyses

### Customer Exploration

* Customer distribution across Brazilian states
* Geographic concentration analysis
* Data quality and missing value checks

### Revenue Analytics

* Monthly revenue trends
* Product category performance
* Payment method analysis
* Customer review score distribution

### RFM Segmentation

Customers are segmented based on:

* **Recency** – How recently they purchased
* **Frequency** – How often they purchase
* **Monetary Value** – How much they spend

This helps identify groups such as:

* Champions
* Loyal Customers
* Potential Loyalists
* At-Risk Customers
* Lost Customers

### K-Means Customer Clustering

Unsupervised machine learning is used to discover behavioral customer groups and compare them against traditional RFM segments.

### Cohort Retention Analysis

Tracks customer retention over time to understand repeat purchase behavior and identify drop-off points in the customer lifecycle.

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

Estimates the long-term value generated by customers and highlights high-value segments for retention strategies.

### Product Affinity Analysis

Identifies commonly purchased product combinations that can support:

* Cross-selling
* Bundling strategies
* Personalized recommendations

## Business Insights

Some of the notable findings include:

* Champion customers contribute a disproportionately large share of revenue.
* Credit cards are the dominant payment method.
* Customer retention drops significantly after the first month.
* Electronics and computer-related categories generate a major portion of revenue.
* Certain customer segments show clear signs of churn risk and can be targeted through win-back campaigns.
